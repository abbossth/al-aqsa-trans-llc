What is DOT Licensing?

The U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) regulates commercial vehicle transportation to ensure safety, compliance, and consumer protection. All commercial carriers engaged in interstate vehicle transportation must be registered with the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) and maintain a valid DOT number.

Our Commitment to Compliance

Al Aqsa Auto Transport works exclusively with carriers that maintain active DOT registration and proper licensing. We verify that all carriers in our network:

  • Hold valid DOT numbers issued by the FMCSA
  • Maintain current motor carrier authority
  • Carry adequate insurance coverage (minimum $1,000,000 liability)
  • Have clean safety records and passing inspection ratings
  • Comply with all federal and state regulations
  • Employ licensed commercial drivers with clean records

Why DOT Licensing Matters

DOT licensing is important because it:

  • Ensures Safety: DOT regulations enforce vehicle maintenance standards, driver qualifications, and safety protocols
  • Provides Accountability: Licensed carriers are tracked and held accountable for their safety record
  • Protects Consumers: DOT registration requires proper insurance and bonding
  • Maintains Standards: Regular inspections and compliance checks ensure industry standards are met

Verifying Carrier Licensing

Before a carrier transports your vehicle, they will provide you with their DOT number. You can verify their licensing and safety record through:

  • Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) website: ai.fmcsa.dot.gov
  • Carrier's website or credentials provided at pickup
  • Our customer service team can provide verification upon request

Insurance Requirements

All DOT licensed carriers must maintain:

  • Primary Liability Insurance: Minimum $1,000,000 for auto transporters
  • Cargo Insurance: Coverage for damage or loss of transported vehicles
  • Contingent Cargo Coverage: Additional protection offered by reputable brokers
